All figures are in millions of dollars over the forward estimates (2013-2017). Most of the figures have been taken from the Liberal Party’s costings document, published yesterday (5 September 2013). Sadly, most mainstream news reporting of the document has done a poor job conveying the scale of all the different measures. Labor have published an equivalent document, but it lists policies that are relatively cheap; all of the major multi-billion-dollar commitments were in the May budget. What's not included?
  • The Liberal document claims a $1.1bn “dividend from stopping the boats”. While this is a reasonable sum for reduced spending on border patrols, it’s not clear that one side will actually stop boat arrivals any more than the other, especially since their policies are so similar.
  • **** Labor has proposed a supplemental job-seeker allowance, which the Liberal document will cancel for $1.1bn in savings, but details have not been released by either side???
  • The Gonski school funding package has been agreed to by both parties.
  • Likewise, some projects have equal funding from both sides even though one or the other has made a bigger deal out of it. For instance, the Pacific Highway upgrade and Gateway WA projects have equal funding commitments up to 2017.
